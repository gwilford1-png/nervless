Getting more support

Nervless is built to help you get comfortable speaking. It isn't a replacement for professional care — and it was never meant to be. If your anxiety reaches beyond speaking, the right next step might be a person, not an app. The good news: you can do both at once.

When it's worth reaching out

A bit of nerves before you speak is normal — that's exactly what the practice is for. But it's worth talking to someone if your anxiety:

  • spreads well beyond speaking, into lots of everyday situations
  • shows up as panic attacks, or physical symptoms you can't seem to settle
  • stops you doing things that matter to you — turning down work, events, or opportunities you'd otherwise want

None of that means something has gone wrong with you. It means an app on its own isn't the right tool — and there are people whose whole job is to help.

Doing both works best

If you take one thing from this page: therapy and practice aren't either/or. A therapist helps you understand and loosen the anxiety; Nervless gives you a safe place to actually do the thing, over and over, until your nervous system stops treating a podium like a threat. The people who get furthest tend to run both at once.

If you're in crisis right now

If things feel unbearable, or you're having thoughts of harming yourself, please reach out now. These lines are free, confidential, and open when you need them.

Samaritans — call 116 123, free, any time of the day or night.
Shout — text SHOUT to 85258 for free 24/7 support by text.
NHS 111 — call 111 and select option 2 for urgent mental health support.
If your life is at immediate risk, call 999.
